My Background

In May 2025, I graduated from Auburn University with a degree in Mechanical Engineering. For as long as I can remember, my plan was to become an engineer. In eighth grade, I was accepted into an engineering magnet program for high school, where I thrived and graduated near the top of my class. My time at Auburn flew by. I gained a wide range of engineering fundamentals, and now I’m eager to apply those skills in a professional setting—while continuing to grow and learn.

What I Love About Engineering

All the typical buzzwords associated with engineering have always resonated with me: Math. Science. Technology. Puzzles. Problem Solving. But it wasn’t until I got to Auburn that I realized engineering is so much more. Every product we interact with—from alarm clocks to zip-lock bags—has been designed to perform perfectly. That’s what I love most: engineering is everywhere, and I’m confident there’s a place where I can help create something great.

Beyond Engineering

Some of my most meaningful accomplishments have taken place outside the classroom. I’ve played the trumpet for over 12 years, including four years in the Auburn University Marching Band and Basketball Pep Band. Our director often said that the soft skills learned in AUMB—teamwork, time management, high standards, and leadership—will carry us forward in any career.

I am also an Eagle Scout, a distinction earned through years of service, leadership, and perseverance. For my Eagle project, I designed and built a trail through Fort Mitchell National Cemetery in Alabama—an experience that taught me as much about project execution as it did about community impact.

What’s Next

Pursuing a degree in engineering has always been my plan, and I’m proud to have achieved it. I chose mechanical engineering because it provides a broad foundation that opens the door to many fields.

As for what’s next, I’m excited to find a position that will allow me to keep learning, while contributing meaningful solutions through the knowledge I already have.
